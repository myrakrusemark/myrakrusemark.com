Future I

The Dark Spiral

In which authoritarian consolidation succeeds, and America becomes the thing it once opposed

Probability estimate: 15-25% based on current trajectories

"U.S. democracy will likely break down during the second Trump administration, in the sense that it will cease to meet standard criteria for liberal democracy."

— Foreign Affairs, "The Path to American Authoritarianism"
2025
The Purge Begins

Mass firings across federal agencies. "Schedule F" reclassifies tens of thousands of civil servants as at-will employees. The DOJ becomes an instrument of political revenge. Investigations opened against political opponents.

The Bright Line Watch survey of political scientists shows democracy rating plummeting from 67 to 55—"the biggest drop since we started tracking."

2026
The Midterms That Weren't

Voting restrictions in key states disenfranchise millions. Gerrymandering locks in Republican majorities. Despite widespread discontent, Democrats fail to retake the House. Claims of fraud delegitimize results in close races.

Scholars had predicted attacks on minority voting rights as "an expected outcome of authoritarian consolidation." They were right.

2027
The Broligarchy Ascendant

Tech billionaires formally merge with government. Musk's companies receive exclusive government contracts. Zuckerberg's platforms become de facto state media. AI surveillance systems deployed "for national security."

The techno-feudalism analysts warned about becomes reality. Corporations don't just influence government—they become it.

2028
The Constitutional Crisis

Trump announces intention to seek third term, citing "unfinished business." Loyal courts decline to intervene. The 22nd Amendment, like so many norms before it, proves to be just words on paper when no one enforces them.

Some analysts had warned: "The damage Trump and his cronies are willing to do makes it unlikely they will risk having to hand back the keys in 2029. They become prisoners of power."

2029-2030
The New Normal

Opposition media shuttered or self-censored. Universities defunded and politicized. Protests met with overwhelming force. Elections continue—but outcomes are predetermined. America has become Hungary writ large.

The "competitive authoritarianism" model is complete: elections exist, but the playing field is so tilted that the opposition can never win.

· · ·
2031-2033
Climate Cooperation Collapses

US withdrawal from climate agreements triggers global cascade. Other nations follow suit. The IPCC's 1.5°C target becomes impossible. We lock in 2.5-3°C warming. Extreme weather events multiply.

The window for preventing catastrophic climate change closes. What comes next is adaptation—for those who can afford it.

2034-2036
The Great Sorting

Internal migration accelerates. Blue-state refugees flee to coasts. Red states entrench. The richest 1% retreat to fortified enclaves. The middle class effectively ceases to exist.

Inequality reaches levels not seen since feudal Europe. 60% of wealth comes from inheritance, cronyism, or monopoly—a "new aristocratic oligarchy."

2037-2040
The AI Panopticon

AI-powered surveillance becomes total. Predictive policing targets dissidents before they act. Social credit systems determine access to services. The "15-year AI dystopia" former Google exec Mo Gawdat predicted is in full swing.

Resistance becomes nearly impossible. Not because people don't want change, but because the systems to organize it have been dismantled or surveilled into silence.

End State: 2040

America is no longer a democracy by any meaningful definition. It's a competitive authoritarian state with the trappings of elections but none of the substance. The global democratic recession has become a depression. Climate change has accelerated past the point of prevention. The window that existed in 2025 has closed.

This future is not inevitable. It requires sustained institutional failure, public acquiescence, and the absence of effective resistance. But it is possible.

"This is authoritarianism on the one hand, but it's an authoritarianism that can be reversed—and I think likely will be reversed."

— Steven Levitsky, co-author of "How Democracies Die"
